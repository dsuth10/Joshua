Lessons from Tracy:
A Modern Guide to Cyclone Safety

Fifty years ago, Darwin was reshaped by the wind. Today, we honor that history by ensuring we are ready when the sirens sound again.

warning_amber Phase I: Before

Alert

The window of opportunity closes fast. When a cyclone watch is issued, time is your most valuable resource. Preparation must be methodical and calm. Ensure your property is clear of loose debris that could become missiles in high winds.

Immediate Actions
  • check_box_outline_blankClear yard of loose objects
  • check_box_outline_blankTape windows in 'X' pattern
  • check_box_outline_blankCheck emergency kit batteries
  • check_box_outline_blankFuel vehicles & generators

cyclone Phase II: During

Critical

When the wind howls, logic must override fear. Stay low and stay inside. The eye of the storm is a deceptive calm—do not be lured outside. Your safest location is a small interior room, hallway, or bathroom devoid of windows.

Survival Protocol
  • radio_button_uncheckedDisconnect all electrical
  • radio_button_uncheckedShelter in smallest room
  • radio_button_uncheckedProtect head with mattress
  • radio_button_uncheckedKeep radio on for alerts

medical_services Phase III: After

Recovery

Silence does not always mean safety. The aftermath brings hidden dangers: live wires, gas leaks, and unstable structures. Wait for the official 'All Clear' from authorities before venturing out to assess damage or help neighbors.

Safe Recovery
  • check_circle_outlineWait for official all-clear
  • check_circle_outlineAvoid fallen power lines
  • check_circle_outlineCheck for gas leaks
  • check_circle_outlinePhotograph damage for insurance
